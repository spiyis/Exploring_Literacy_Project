About the Project

This project is a continuation of a final projected I created in DATA 303: Data Visualization at William & Mary. The original project displayed visualizations created in Python and Tableau onto a Google Site. To expand my understanding of data visualization, I adapted my existing visualizations to fit onto my own webpage created using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. This webpage is the product of my independent study of the programming languages.

I decided to delve into literacy and reading preferences, as the subject is of personal importance to me. I have always been an avid reader; I spent much of my childhood curled up with my cat, reading a good book (always fiction!). While studying Data Science at William & Mary, I found myself missing fiction, and moreover missing having the time to read! I also learned that I have a passion for literacy, especially in regards to equal access to education, both in the United States and abroad. I believe data visualization can be a powerful tool for education, showcasing complex information in a comprehnsive and digestible manner. Finding a way to blend my love of literature and my learning in Data Science has been the highlight of my work on this project.

Above you see just some of my favorite books. They serve as the underlying inspiration behind this project, as my love for reading drove my initial exploration into the Goodreads app.


While completing this project, I decided to track just how much I was reading. For the months I worked on the project (January 2024-May 2024), my reading time flunctuated greatly. I found that I read much more when I started a new series, as I did not stop reading until I completed it. Once I finished the three-book series, Crescent City by Sarah J. Maas, my reading time went back down, but never as far as when I started tracking in Janurary. Oftentimes, my desire to start reading again is kickstarted by a good book!
